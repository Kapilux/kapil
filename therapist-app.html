Therapist App

An application aimed at assisting therapists in treating children with autism

1.5 months
Lead Product Designer
Product | Tab

Overview

The following case study outlines the design and development of an application aimed at assisting therapists in treating children with autism. The app allows therapists to monitor a child's progress as they complete tasks designed to improve brain development and function. App enable therapists to closely examine a child's behavior and responses during specific stimuli and activities within a VR or natural environment.

My Role: I was part of the project as a Lead product designer and was responsible for UX/UI Design.

Collaboration: Business Stakeholder, Project Manager, Team Lead, and Dev team.

Tools: Sketch, Jira, MS Teams

Research

This application is primarily designed for therapists who specialize in working with children with autism. These therapists require tools that provide them with comprehensive data and control over the therapeutic process to effectively monitor progress and adjust treatment plans. We collaborated closely with the therapist to identify pain points and expectations. We deep dived into deeper understanding of the application, usage and users.

Target Group

Therapist

They need to moniter data for progress and adjust treatment plans.

Caregivers

They will use to moniter and update data while working in home enviornment.

Pain Points

Based on the information provided by the therapist and stakeholders, therapists face several pain points in traditional therapy sessions, which this app aims to address:

  • Difficulty in customizing the patient experience: Therapists need the ability to tailor tasks to suit specific needs.
  • Limited data access: Struggle to quickly access historical data and relevant information during sessions.
  • Lack of objective progress tracking: Traditional methods may lack the detailed, objective data needed to accurately track a patient's progress.
  • Challenges in maintaining patient motivation: Difficult to keep children with autism engaged and motivated throughout the therapy process.
  • Inefficient data management: Managing and accessing various forms of patient data.
  • Difficulty in assessing patient's emotional state: Therapists need tools to help them understand a child's comfort levels, anxiety, and social progress.

Benchmark Study

To understand more about features and important aspect of the treatment, we started on benchmark study to gain more knowledge. We identified three imporant aspects of treatment therapist heavily rely on.

Heart Rate Variability (HRV)
HRV is important to monitor stress, recovery and overall well-being.
Head/Gaze Tracking
Therapist observe patient eye/head movement to observe their attention or interest.
Speech Modulation
Therapist observe pitch, volume, pace and tone of patients to check their public speaking on topics.

Opportunities

During our research we identify several opportunities to enhance autism therapy and address the identified pain points:

  • Customizable patient experience: Allow therapists to adjust various aspects of therapy, tailoring it to the specific needs and goals of each child.
  • Accessible historical data: Relevant historical data can be readily available, enabling therapists to make informed decisions.
  • Objective progress tracking: Provide detailed data on a child's performance, including head/gaze tracking and speech patterns, allowing for more objective progress measurement
  • Increased patient engagement: Gamification elements, such as avatar-based reviews, story-lines, goals, and rewards, can be incorporated into the app to enhance patient motivation and engagement.
  • Streamlined data access: Centralize access to various data formats, such as audio files and video sessions, improving efficiency.
  • Enhanced assessment of emotional state: Head/gaze tracking features that can provide insights into a child's comfort levels, anxiety, and social progress.
  • Improved speech modulization: Tools to analyze and provide feedback on speech patterns, helping therapists address speech-related challenges.
  • Course monitoring: Control and adjustment of various aspects of the therapy session, such as the pace and repetition of questions, to optimize the experience for each child.

Ideation

Based on our research findings, we started on the ideation to Feature MVP, IA, wireframes and visual design. We iteratively refined these designs through multiple feedback sessions with stakeholders and users.

Approach

We started with a strong focus on addressing the pain points identified through user research, incorporating features such as:

Overall Features
  • Tailored patient dashboard with relevant the goals/needs of the patient.
  • Historical data that is relevant to the patient’s goal should be seen within the first views of the dashboard and easily seen/accessible.
  • Gamification throughout the experience is important to the patient maintaining motivation. Therefore, showcases patient scores and achievements on the first screen of the dashboard can remind the medical professional to call out these achievements to foster patient motivation.
  • Further documents like audio files or video sessions should be easily accessible to the medical professional at any time.
  • Onboarding of the patient with the medical professional can ensure the patient dashboard is tailored and relevant to the goals/needs of the patient.
  • Basic functions include not just pause, play, and repeat videos.
Features related to HRV
  • Seeing an overview of statistics is not enough. The medical professional should be able to access additional information within one click from the overview graph.
  • Actionable feedback/insights are a growing trend in reporting and can help a medical professional initiate change or discuss details with the patient quickly.
  • Notifications such as an increase or reduction of the environment can help the medical professional mitigate anxiety using data such as heart rate.
  • Further documents like audio files or video sessions should be easily accessible to the medical professional at any time.
  • Comparing the patients data to collective group ranges to serve as a baseline may help in decision making and quick understanding of the patient’s overall progress.
Features related to Head/Gaze Tracking
  • Fixation, heatmaps, and use of ratio would be most helpful in understanding the behavior behind the child’s eye movements to imply comfort levels, and/or anxiety, and social progress.
    • Fixation can explain the time it takes for a person to look at something after a stimulus
    • Heatmaps can easily show this fixation time using simple visuals
    • The use of ratios can break down a full visual scene into segments which can be evaluated for the amount of time spent in a certain segment.
  • Evaluating the sequence of eye movements will yield data to see in which order a patient views characters after audibly hearing stimuli.
Features related to Speech Modulation
  • Help with speech modulization, show a target range that the user should aim to be in. This can be shown in real time to the therapist or after the testing is complete.
  • The therapist can select what type of speech pattern the AI should report on ex. pace, articulation, intonation, etc.
  • Short mini exercises can be included to have the patient re-work on the same phrase until deemed correct.
  • Provide an overall speech score to help the user see quickly progress today and progress overtime. The therapist should also be able to read a transcript and see the history of past speeches. Sharing to other devices or individuals is encouraged.

Task Flow

We started working on MVP and future features to be integrated. These features were discussed with stakeholders to agree and get confirmation. Sharing this early structure with users helped us validate how they expected to navigate the system—ensuring we were on the right track before moving into detailed design.

UI Design Phase

Project was focused on getting MVP designed and developed first. The wireframing and visual design were heavily influenced by user feedback, with iterative design and constructive feedbacks though click-through prototype helped us in ensuring the product meets the needs and expectations of the target users. The focus was on creating an intuitive, efficient, and visually appealing interface that supports seamless workflows.

Conclusion

By addressing these pain points and leveraging the opportunities presented by VR technology, this application has the potential to significantly improve the effectiveness and efficiency of autism therapy.
