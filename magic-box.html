Sprint Magic Box companion app

Setup assistant setup app for award winning Sprint product

1 months
Lead Product Designer
Mobile

Overview

Sprint Magic Box, Think of it as a friendly little device that took the existing Sprint cellular signal and gave it a real boost inside your home. Instead of wandering around trying to find a bar, the Magic Box amplified that signal, creating a stronger and more reliable wireless connection where you needed it most. It was all about making sure you could actually use your phone properly when you were inside.

My Role: I was part of the project as a Lead product designer and was responsible for UX/UI Design.

Collaboration: Business Stakeholder, Project Manager, and Dev team.

Tools: Sketch, Jira, MS Teams

Discovery

In the telecommunications landscape, a common customer pain point is poor cellular reception within buildings. This issue arises from various factors, including building materials that obstruct signal penetration, distance from cell towers, and network congestion. Sprint made an app that made setting up their Magic Box super simple. It guided you through connecting it to your Wi-Fi and let you know what was happening. Basically, it was all about getting you a much better phone signal indoors, even if it was usually terrible.

For Sprint customers, most common problems were:

  • Dropped calls: Interruptions in voice communication, leading to frustration and the need to repeat conversations.
  • Slow data speeds: Difficulty accessing the internet, streaming media, or using data-dependent applications.
  • Missed communications: Inability to receive important calls or messages, potentially impacting productivity and personal connections.
  • Customer dissatisfaction: Overall negative perception of the network provider due to unreliable service.
Implementation and Impact

The introduction of the Magic Box and its companion app aimed to provide a seamless and user-friendly solution to a pervasive problem. The expected outcomes included:

  • Improved indoor coverage: Significantly enhanced signal strength and reliability within homes and businesses.
  • Enhanced customer experience: Reduced dropped calls, faster data speeds, and more consistent connectivity.
  • Increased customer satisfaction: Improved perception of Sprint's network quality and a greater sense of reliability.
  • Reduced customer churn: Decreased likelihood of customers switching to other providers due to poor indoor coverage.
  • Lower support costs: A streamlined setup process through the app likely reduced the number of customer support calls related to installation issues.

App features

This application is used to assist users in the installation and configuration of Sprint devices:

  • One unified app for Sprint Magic Box TREBL, SILVER and PEBBLE.
  • Free and easy to download.
  • Provides installation progress during the device Set-up process.
  • Provides device status and configuration information.
  • Allows user to change backhaul to Wi-Fi/Ethernet for Pebble.
  • Provides links to product Help and FAQ Pages

Ideation

While specific quantitative data on the program's overall success may vary, the general impact of the Sprint Magic Box can be assessed through:

  • User-friendly setup: The companion app played a vital role in simplifying the installation process and improving user adoption.
  • Simplifying installation: The app provided a step-by-step guide to help users through the setup process, ensuring correct placement and configuration of the Magic Box.
  • Proactive solutions for customer pain points: By addressing a common frustration, Sprint showed a commitment to improving customer experience.
  • Customer feedback: Reports on improved call quality, data speeds, and overall satisfaction with indoor coverage.
  • Reduction in support inquiries: Decreased volume of customer service calls related to indoor coverage problems.
  • Network performance data: Measurements of signal strength and network performance in areas where Magic Boxes were deployed.
  • Providing status updates: The app kept users informed about the device's status, signal strength, and any potential issues.

Design Phase

Developing a clean, intuitive, and trustworthy visual design that aligns with the insurance provider's brand. This includes:

  • Clear Visual Hierarchy: Ensuring important information, like the AI's suggestions and confidence scores, is easily scannable.
  • Consistent Design Patterns: Utilizing familiar UI elements and interaction patterns to enhance usability.
  • Accessibility: Adhering to accessibility guidelines (WCAG) to ensure the search is usable by everyone.
  • Feedback Mechanisms: Providing clear and timely feedback to the user throughout the search process (e.g., loading states, success messages, error handling).

FAQ Scereens

FAQs are segmented into different categories instead of a single page. This approach aims to lower the cognitive load and make it easier for users to find the information they need.

Conclusion

The Sprint Magic Box and its companion app represented a strategic effort to address the challenge of poor indoor cellular coverage. By providing a user-friendly solution that enhanced signal strength and improved connectivity, Sprint aimed to increase customer satisfaction, reduce churn, and improve its overall network perception. This case study highlights the importance of understanding customer needs and leveraging technology to deliver effective solutions to common service issues.
