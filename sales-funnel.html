Sales Funnel Optimization

Optimization of insurance sales funnel for mobile users.

1 month
Lead UX Designer
Mobile

Overview

We optimized sales funnel for users buying small business insurance. The goal was to make the experience more intuitive and increase conversion for mobile users. We already did optimization on payment page which resulted in 10% increase in conversion. This was further optimization proposed to increase conversion on overall funnel.

My Role: I was part of the project as a Lead UX designer and was responsible for the overall UX design and strategy.

Key Outcomes

10% increase in payment converison

Reason for optimization

  • Low conversion rate: 8.7% conversion rate on mobile compare to 19.8% on desktop.
  • High Drop-off rate: more than 23% drop-off on very first step.
  • Payment Conversion rate: With one of the experiment we saw 10% increase in payment converison
  • Growing number of users: Mobile users are growing and poor experience is resulting in low CTR.

Research

Analyzing user sessions: I went through Tealeaf live sessions to understand user behaviour while using site on mobile.

Campaign analytics data: Deep-dive into Paid campaigns data to observe user journey and drop-off steps.

Heatmaps & Eye tracking: To understand areas where user were focusing and which areas they were ignoring.

Competitve Analysis

We also deep-dived into the market to understand approach of other insurance companies

Problem Statement

Outcome of the analysis of the various data points I identified major friction points for users:

  • Persistent usability issues: Heatmap shows Users struggle to find relevant product info.
  • Unclear or Confusing Navigation: Users struggle to find relevant step info.
  • Content heirarchy: Users feel overwhelmed or unsure why certain details are needed early on.
  • Long scroll: Forms ask for too much information on very first step.
  • Design and layout: Frustrated by UI friction or unable to complete tasks easily on mobile.
  • Touch targets: Tiny touch targets and slow-loading content on mobile
  • Trust and Credibility: Higher drop-off rates on mobile vs desktop; session time 20–30% lower on mobile.

Ideation

Based on our research findings, we decided to test a optmized mobile version of sales funnel.

Progressive Profiling
Break down lengthy forms in logical steps
Visibility of system status
Clearly define where user is in the process.
Clear Calls-to-Action
Prominent CTA display and easily accessible throughout the funnel.
Trust and Credibility
Highlight real time support for immediate assistance.

High Fidelity Wireframes

After reviewing all the user insights, goals, site architecture, and behavioral data, I began designing high-fidelity wireframes. Every decision in the sales funnel layout was driven by data and aimed at improving the overall user experience.

Product selection & Personal info

  • Step Decoupling: Research showed users were hesitant to provide personal details upfront. To address this, I restructured the funnel so users first select a product, then enter personal information—building trust and reducing drop-offs.
  • Touch Targets: Applied thumb-accessibility principles by designing larger CTAs and placing them within easy reach to enhance usability on mobile devices.
  • Iconography: Introduced meaningful icons for each product category to help users quickly identify relevant options and reduce cognitive load.
  • Help & contact: Made contact information prominently visible to reassure users and strengthen trust signals throughout the journey.
  • Progress Stepper: Included a intuitive visual stepper to clearly show users where they are in the process, making the journey more transparent and less overwhelming.

Quote Journey

  • Contextual Loader: Replaced the generic loader with a contextual message explaining that quotes are being generated, including a brief summary of what's happening in the background to set expectations and build trust.
  • Grouped Policy Summary: Organized key quote details—like cover amount, premium, and excess—into a grouped, easy-to-scan summary for each policy, helping users compare options more effectively.
  • Sticky CTA Button: Introduced a sticky “Next” and “View Summary” button that remains visible after policy selection, reducing friction and keeping the next step always accessible.
  • Dedicated Start Date Step: Added a separate step for users to choose their policy start date, paired with a clear message indicating the quote is saved and protected for 30 days—reinforcing trust and flexibility.
  • Clean, Intuitive Layout: Streamlined the page layout to reduce clutter and cognitive load, making it easier for users to navigate, digest information, and make confident decisions.

Payment Process

To enhance the user experience and improve conversion, we redesigned the payment step by drawing insights from a previous experiment that led to a 10% increase in conversions. The key change involved breaking the long, scroll-heavy payment page into three manageable accordion-style sections.

  • Accordion Structure: The payment process was divided into clear sections (e.g., Legal information, acknowledgements and payment), allowing users to focus on one step at a time without being overwhelmed by the entire form at once.
  • Single-View Focus: Users interacted with just one section at a time, reducing cognitive load and making the experience feel lighter and more guided.
  • Edit Anytime: Users could expand and edit any section at any point, offering flexibility and control without disrupting the flow.
  • Error Reduction: Fewer errors were observed due to task isolation—users were more accurate when completing smaller, focused steps.
  • Expectation Setting: This layout also helped set clear expectations, showing users exactly how many steps were involved, which contributed to lower abandonment rates.
