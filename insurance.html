Project Overview

My role: Sr. UX/UI Designer

BizCover NZ provides a digital platform that helps small businesses in New Zealand compare and buy business insurance online. BizCover's website redesign aim was to transform the user experience of NZ/AU premier business insurance comparison platform. The project aimed to improve user engagement, streamline the insurance selection process, and enhance conversion rates through user-centered design principles.

Challenges

The existing website failed to effectively convert visitors into leads, resulting in decreased revenue and increased marketing costs.Users were abandoning the site before completing quote requests, directly impacting business growth.

  • Low user engagement and high bounce rates
  • Suboptimal mobile experience
  • High customer acquisition cost
  • Poor Performance: 14.9 sec page load speed

How might we?

"How might we transform the insurance website into an intuitive, engaging platform that guides users seamlessly through their insurance journey while driving measurable business results?"

Research & Discovery

Discovery Workshop

The discovery workshop was a focused get-together with everyone involved to figure out exactly what we're doing with this project, why it matters to the business, and how we'd know if we are in right direction.

Problem Statement from Business POV

The discovery workshop revealed key challenges across business, user, and technical areas.

From a user standpoint, the dated interface and poor mobile experience made the brand feel out-of-touch, while the site was not fully optimised for New Zealand users.

Technically, the website suffered from a 19-second load time and poor performance scores (44 desktop, 26 mobile). Content updates were slow and dependent on developers, limiting agility.

These findings underscored the need for a complete UX overhaul to modernise design, enhance performance, and improve both customer experience and operational efficiency.

Problems & Patterns

As we analysed the data, genuine user insights began to emerge-forming a solid base for thoughtful, user-centric design decisions. Using both qualitative and quantitative inputs from diverse sources, we mapped out the core struggles users faced.

Insurance Literacy

Lack of awareness about the risk and insurance protection

Navigation Structure

Users find Navigation menu confusing and hard to navigate

Visual Hierarchy

The site felt functional and transactional, but lacked engagement

Trust & Credibility

Lack of trust indicators like testimonials or ratings or even upfront pricing info

Price Sensitive

Customers are more inclined towards pricing before getting into the funnel

Content Overload

Long, excessive and poorly organized content leading to frustration and drop-offs

Mobile Experience

Site was not optimized for mobile users leading to major drop-offs

Accessibility Issues

Some sections of website were failing accessibility

Mapping & Prioritization

Mapping encourages open, creative thinking where all ideas-big or small-are explored without judgment. It helps teams uncover innovative solutions and challenge assumptions. Prioritization brings structure by evaluating each finding based on impact, feasibility, and alignment with project goals.

Hypothesis & Goals

The development of the website is guided by the following principles to ensure user value and effectiveness.

Simplified Navigation

Group links in a way so that it is easy for user to find relevant information on the screen.

Clear Value Proposition

Clearly communicate the unique value proposition of the business insurance offerings.

Personalization

Recommendations & customizations to enhance user engagement & satisfaction.

Building User Trust

Displaying trust indicators to reinforce credibility and reliability.

Mobile Optimization

Responsive design principles to adapt the layout and features across different screen sizes.

Brainstroming & Ideation

Based on the research findings and prioritised insights, we conducted brainstorming sessions to explore multiple design concepts addressing key user pain points. These collaborative sessions involved close discussions with stakeholders during weekly check-ins, ensuring that business goals and user needs stayed aligned. Through this iterative process, several wireframe ideas were developed, refined, and validated collectively‚Äîforming the foundation for the final design direction.

Design Logic and Key Considerations

  • Mobile-first Approach
  • Sticky in-page navigation for easy access to sections
  • Relevant Imagery
  • Simplifying jargons with business needs

Final Wireframes

The final wireframes represent the refined outcome of multiple design iterations and stakeholder feedback. Each element was carefully aligned with user insights, business goals, and accessibility standards to ensure a seamless and intuitive experience.

Visual Design & Design System

The final visual design translated the approved wireframes into a cohesive, modern interface that balanced aesthetics with usability. The visual language focused on clarity, consistency, and accessibility, ensuring users could navigate key workflows with ease. A clean color palette, readable typography, and thoughtful use of whitespace helped create a professional and approachable experience that aligned with the brand‚Äôs tone and purpose.

Mobile-First Approach

This approach ensured that the most critical user journeys‚Äîsuch as viewing KPIs, tracking progress, and performing quick actions‚Äîremained intuitive and fast-loading on mobile devices. Every component was optimised for touch interaction, legibility, and adaptive spacing, providing a seamless experience across varying screen sizes and orientations.

Responsive Desktop Designs

Grid-based structures, scalable typography, and adaptive components allowed the interface to adjust effortlessly across devices. The desktop views provided enhanced visibility for coverage, gamification, and broader features, maintaining consistency while optimising for productivity and clarity.

Token-Based Design System

Design tokens defined key attributes such as color, typography, spacing, and component states, creating a single source of truth for both designers and developers. This systematic approach enabled faster iteration, easier theming, and improved collaboration-ensuring that future design updates remain consistent, accessible, and efficient to implement.
