Electronic Health Record Application

A bilingual patient-facing healthcare app consolidating medical records into an intuitive, accessible interface—delivered.

Project Overview

My role: Sr. UX Designer

This project involved designing a healthcare application for Arab Health. The goal was to create a bilingual (English/Arabic) patient-facing experience that consolidates medical records such as encounters, lab results, medications, and documents. The work was delivered in collaboration with Malaffi and Orion Health, using an agile, iterative design approach with weekly reviews via AxureRP.

Challenges

Simplify a complex healthcare system without losing functionality, while supporting two languages with fundamentally different text directions (LTR & RTL), and delivering a demo-ready prototype in a compressed timeline.

User Needs & Pain Points

Based on content audit and stakeholder interviews, we identified core user needs and opportunities for improvement.

High Cognitive Load

Complex medical data without visual hierarchy makes it difficult to identify priorities and find information quickly.

Unclear Navigation

Fragmented workflows cause users to struggle finding specific records and lose focus during multi-step tasks.

Jargon-Heavy Language

Medical terminology reduces user confidence and creates uncertainty about health information accuracy.

No Bilingual Support

Lack of Arabic option creates accessibility barriers for multilingual users and limits market reach.

What Users Need:

  • Quick, easy access to medical history without cognitive overload
  • Clear, non-technical language that builds confidence and trust
  • Seamless navigation between different medical record sections
  • Transparent language switching between English and Arabic

Key Insights:

  • Content accuracy and simplification of medical terminology are critical to user trust
  • Arabic support requires RTL (right-to-left) layout and early design consideration
  • Prototype must be demo-ready, not production-ready, requiring prioritisation
  • Tight timeline demands agile collaboration with weekly stakeholder reviews

Design Strategy

Our design approach focused on simplifying complex healthcare data while maintaining accuracy and supporting bilingual workflows.

Clarity Over Aesthetics

Every design decision prioritises clear communication of medical information and user confidence.

Progressive Disclosure

Present essential information first, with drill-down access to detailed data to avoid cognitive overload.

Modular & Scalable

Consistent component patterns across Encounters, Labs, Medications, and other medical record sections.

Bilingual Ready

All screens designed with RTL support from the start-not as an afterthought.

Information Architecture

Key Design Elements

Card-Based Layout

Each medical record presented as a scannable card with essential info and drill-down access

Clear Visual Hierarchy

Consistent use of size, colour, spacing to guide attention to key information

Empty States & Guidance

Thoughtfully designed empty states to reassure users and provide next steps

RTL Support

All UI components mirrored for Arabic—buttons, icons, navigation—without compromising the English experience
