Website Revamp- Business Insurance for SME's

Complete UX overhaul of a legacy banking website, resulting in 85% increase in CTR for desktop and 157% increase in CTR for mobile.

4 Months
Lead UX Designer
Website Link

Overview

The primary goal of website revamp was to transform the existing website into a high-performing platform that effectively engages users, provides a seamless and intuitive experience, and significantly increases the number of users who proceed to compare insurance quotes. This involved a comprehensive website revamp, incorporating user-centered design principles and a data-driven approach, to address key challenges such as low user engagement, high bounce rates, and a suboptimal mobile experience. The ultimate objective was to drive business growth by improving lead generation and reducing the cost per acquisition.

My Role: I was part of the project as a Lead UX designer and was responsible for the overall user experience strategy and execution.

Collaboration: Business Stakeholder, Project Manager, Sales and Marketing Team, Business Analyst, Sr. UI Designer and Dev team.

Tools: Miro, Figma, Jira, Trello, MS Teams

Key Outcomes

+ 85% increase in Desktop CTR
+ 157% increase in Mobile CTR
- 28% reduction in Customer Care Calls
- 62.5% reduction in Bounce rate

Research & Discovery

We kicked off the research phase by diving deep into understanding our users - their pain points, motivations, and what opportunities we could tap into. We used mix of methods like user interviews, NPS surveys, competitive analysis, and reviewing analytics data to get a full picture. This approach helped uncover real insights that set a strong foundation for making smart, user-centered design decisions. We gained insights through data from Google Analytics, Tealeaf, Hotjar and PowerBi (Annual Data) and double-jacking customer calls sessions with sales agents to understand user struggles.

Purpose of research was to identify:

  • Understand the friction points and struggle for users while buying a insurance online
  • Analyize quant data to identify patterns, trends and understand metrics
  • Identify other competitors for the same domain. Evaluate their strenghts and weaknesses
  • Understand the market trends across the insurance industry for best practices
  • Learn about opportunities and motivations that will help in creating seamless exerience for users

While talking and listening to users, we uncovered major pain-points

We conducted few user and agent talking sessions to really get into user shoes — what they’re struggling with, what they care about, and what they actually need. These conversations gave a ton of honest insights and uncovered patterns that helped in focusing our UX/UI efforts right from the start.

  • Website is a bit clunky, buggy and need improvement.
  • Navigation is hard to use and understand.
  • Some users find site a bit confusing and had trouble buying insurance.
  • New business owners feel videos of explaining different insurance would be nice.
  • No examples of historic claims settlement makes user hard to trust.
  • Site didn't work well on mobile and inforamtion was hard to find.
  • The platform response time to move between sections/screens takes too long.
  • Few users mentioned there's a lot to decide and understand so product explanation could be improved.

Analytics, Heatmaps and User Sessions Analysis

Along with user and agent interactions, We dug into analytics, heatmaps, and session recordings to understand how people were actually interacting with the site. This helped in identifying where users were dropping off, getting stuck, or just behaving differently than we expected. Combining real data with user feedback gave us a much clearer picture of what was working, what wasn’t, and where the biggest opportunities for improvement were.

Analytics- Google, PowerBi

  • Only 21% of users go to the product selection
  • 17% user go back to product pages looking for more info
  • 70% professionals interact with us on desktop
  • 60% tradies use mobile for insurance
  • Mobile drop off is higher than desktop (60% vs 55%)
  • Identified Occupations experiencing major drop off

Heatmaps- Hotjar

  • Users are ignoring text based sections
  • More focused areas are Cost and the level of cover
  • User interested in watching videos
  • Most of the users are following F-pattern for reading
  • Image & text sections are working better
  • Trust indicators at the bottom of page not getting much attention

Session Observation- Tealeaf

  • Pages are quite long, most users stop scrolling halfway through
  • Users clicking for more info shows most are still in the research phase
  • Product information placement is making hard for user to stay on page
  • CTA missing across the page resulting in frustation for user
  • Personalization is missing

Competitive Analysis

To get more closer look to NZ insurance market and do some benchmarking we did competitive analysis research across various insurance companies. The analysis was focused on the design, content, and user experience of competitiors website, highlighting strengths and weaknesses. The companies analyzed include Gallagher, State, Tower Insurance, AMI Insurance, and Cactus Insurance.

Insurer Strengths Weakness
  • Clean Design: The site features a clean header navigation and consistent design across pages, enhancing user experience.
  • Content Clarity: Short and clear content with a well-defined hierarchy makes information easy to digest.
  • Engagement Features: The use of one-liner details and expand icons is engaging and intuitive.
  • Product Presentation: Product offerings are presented in an accordion format to reduce cognitive load and avoid scrolling.
  • Dated Imagery: Some imagery appears retro and inconsistent, which may affect user perception.
  • Accessibility: Certain text elements do not meet accessibility standards, potentially hindering usability.
  • User Empathy: Real-life imagery is used to empathize with users, creating a relatable experience.
  • Content Structure: Well-structured content sections with a minimalistic layout enhance readability.
  • Trust Indicators: Awards and recognitions are showcased to build trust and credibility.
  • Ease of Use: The site focuses on ease of use, with a casual tone and unique CTA colors.
  • Product Information: There is limited information on products, which may mislead users entering the funnel.
  • Iconography: Icons require more focus to improve user interaction.
  • Brand Awareness: High brand awareness in the NZ market, supported by awards and recognitions.
  • Visual Consistency: Consistent visual concepts across landing pages enhance brand recognition.
  • Information Layout: Easy-to-consume information presented in table layouts improves user understanding.
  • Iconography: Icons have an outdated look, which may detract from the modernity of the site.
  • Trust Indicators: Trust and credibility indicators could be more prominently displayed.
  • Product Accessibility: Easy access to browse products relevant to specific industries.
  • Coverage Clarity: Coverage is easy to understand with icons and small text.
  • Branding: Illustrations are part of the branding theme, enhancing visual appeal.
  • Content Alignment: Center-aligned content is hard to read and affects usability.
  • White Space: Ineffective use of white space can hinder user navigation.
  • UI Issues: Various UI issues, including line height spacing and capital headings, need attention.
  • Personalization: Real imagery conveys personalization, enhancing user connection.
  • Trust Building: Trust indicators are repeated throughout the page to reinforce credibility.
  • Content Localization: Localized content and USP highlights improve user engagement.
  • Efficiency: Users can enter the funnel with one click, improving efficiency.
  • Content Length: Minimal content may not provide sufficient information for users.
  • Repetitive CTA: CTA repetition could be streamlined for better user experience.

Principles and Solutions

The development of the website is guided by the following principles to ensure user value and effectiveness:

Quick Access to Key Sections
Introduction of sticky sub-navigation bar in header that will allow user to access all sections with one-click.
Clear Product Categorization
Grouping products based on requirements for user to easy to understand.
Simplified Navigation by Industry
Grouping occupation based on industry for user to have easy access to information.
Engaging and Informative Content
Adding a savings section to capture attention and highlight benefits
Building User Trust
Displaying trust indicators to reinforce credibility and reliability.
Reduced Cognitive Load
Structuring information to make content easier to scan and understand.
Enhanced Readability and Accessibility
Use plain language and avoid jargon to improve accessibility.
Optimized Call-to-Action Placement
Placing CTAs strategically throughout the journey.
