CRM Application

Unified customer experience management and opportunity management system.

1.5 months
Lead Product Designer
Product | Web | Mobile

Overview

We developed this for Europe based client- Iptor. This Unified Platform offers a consistent and user-friendly experience for teams across organization. Organizations can leverage a centralized platform for communication, task management, project collaboration, and more, fostering seamless teamwork and improved productivity. CRM is available for deployment of customers choice either Cloud or On-premise. Our design agency was consulted to transform their digital customer experience.

My Role: I was part of the project as a Lead product designer and was responsible for UX/UI Design.

Collaboration: Business Stakeholder, Project Manager, Team Lead, and Dev team.

Tools: Figma, Jira, MS Teams

Impact

40% increase in overall efficiencies

Research

We collaborated closely with the client to identify user pain points and expectations. Through a series of stakeholder and user discovery workshops, we deep dived into deeper understanding of the application, usage and users. The client provided valuable assistance and insights, enabling us to fully understand the product's purpose and its intended market impact.

User Persona

Following an analysis of user behavior and mental models, we developed user personas to represent the primary user groups:

Sales Team

Utilize the product to support the sales process, add opportunities, view opportunity status, getting approvals and customer details.

Back-Office Administrators

Use the product to add customer, customer details and assigning sales person

Management Team

Use product to check status of deals, giving approvals and leverage reports generated by the product to inform key business decisions.

Pain Points

Before diving into design solutions, we took time to understand the real challenges users were facing. We identified several friction areas that were impacting productivity, usability, and overall satisfaction for both field users and admin staff. These pain points helped shape our priorities and guided key decisions throughout the redesign process.

  • The onboarding process for customers was a major challenge for admins, often causing delays and frustration.
  • Admins and managers waste time navigating the system, leading to inefficiency and missed opportunities for quick decision-making.
  • Managers struggled with the old approval process, finding it hard to review details and give feedback or approval smoothly.
  • Users were confused, leading to frustration and slower task completion.
  • Salespeople needing mobile access to the CRM to update info on the go and have faced limitations or difficulty in accessing necessary data from the field.
  • Users are facing difficulty navigating through the application.
  • The dashboard might have been too complex or cluttered, making it hard for users to quickly find key information or track important metrics.
  • Managers have struggled to quickly extract actionable insights from reports.

Ideation

Based on our research findings, we started on the ideation to create journey map, Feature MVP, IA, wireframes and visual design. We iteratively refined these designs through multiple feedback sessions with stakeholders and users.

Journey Mapping

I created a journey map outlines the typical day of a staff and how they manage their daily tasks and patient care. The journey map gave us a broad view of how users interact with the product and business at different points. It helped us steps involved, find friction points, find opportunities, and tackle those pain points.

  • Admin: How admin journey starts with adding customer into the system and assign it to sales person.
  • Sales person: How sales person journey starts recieving the opportunity and consulting customer.
  • Approver: How approver journey starts after recieving the opportunity details from sales person.

Feature Mapping- MVP

We created a feature map to decide what should go into the MVP, focusing on the essentials—features that truly mattered to users and aligned with business goals. It kept us on track, helped avoid unnecessary add-ons, and made sure we were building something valuable while staying within the defined scope and timeline.

Information Architecture

I created a draft Information Architecture (IA) to map out how key features and content would be organized. Sharing this early structure with users helped us validate how they expected to navigate the system—ensuring we were on the right track before moving into detailed design.

UI Design Phase

Project timeline were very tight, due to this time constraint we took the paper sketche wireframe approach. This helped us quickly putting out quick and dirty ideas on paper. The wireframing and visual design were heavily influenced by user feedback, with iterative design and constructive feedbacks though click-through prototype helped us in ensuring the product meets the needs and expectations of the target users. The focus was on creating an intuitive, efficient, and visually appealing interface that supports seamless workflows.
