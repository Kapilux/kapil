Adaptive Mobile- Insurance Product landing page

Mobile focused landing page for a insurance product, resulting in 20% increase in CTR .

2 weeks
Lead UX Designer
Mobile

Overview

I designed an adaptive landing page for the insurance product, tailoring the layout and messaging based on user behavior and device type. The goal was to make the experience feel more personalized and focused. After launching the new design as an experiment, we saw a 20% uplift in conversions — showing that small, smart changes in structure and content can make a big impact.

My Role: I was part of the project as a Lead UX designer and was responsible for the overall UX design and strategy.

Key Outcomes

20% increase in CTR
33% reduction in Bounce rate

Reason for optimization

  • Low conversion rate: 48% conversion rate on mobile compare to 84% on desktop.
  • High bounce rate: 28% bounce rate on mobile compare to 19% on desktop.
  • Growing number of users: With mobile users surpassing desktop poor experience is resulting in low CTR.

Research

Analyzing user sessions: I went through Tealeaf live sessions to understand user behaviour while using site on mobile.

Campaign analytics data: Deep-dive into Paid campaigns data to observe user journey and drop-off steps.

Heatmaps & Eye tracking: To understand areas where user were focusing and which areas they were ignoring.

Problem Statement

Outcome of the analysis of the various data points we I identified major friction points for users:

  • Persistent usability issues: Heatmap shows users are missing USP’s and CTA.
  • Lack of value propositions: Trust and credibility are not highlighted enough.
  • Content heirarchy: Users struggle to find key sections or information.
  • Long scroll: Most of the sections on page remain inattentive due to long scroll. Users don’t have a clue what information is available on page.
  • Design and layout: The page is content heavy that looks cluttered and unprofessional.
  • Touch targets: Are not as per mobile layout guidelines.

Ideation

Based on our research findings, we decided to test a adaptive mobile design. This separate page will help us in targeting mobile customers and at the same time reduce development effort on the main landing page. Major driving factors for this experiment are:

Clear Value Proposition
Clearly communicate the unique value proposition of the business.
Content Hierarchy
Optimize the content hierarchy to ensure that content is logically organized and easy to access
Navigation
Group links in a way so that it is easy for user to find relevant information on the screen.
Clear Calls-to-Action
Ensure that CTAs are prominently displayed and easily accessible throughout the website.

High Fidelity Wireframes

Going through all insights of the user, user goals, the architecture, and user behaviour, I started working on high-fidelity wireframes making data-informed decisions on how landing page would look like.

Above the fold

  • Sticky sub-navigation bar in header that will allow user to find key sections or information.
  • Clear and compelling CTA
  • Grouping occupation based on industry for user to have easy access to information
  • Highlighting the USP's for trust and credibility
  • Addressing the accessibility issues for users with low vision or impairments
  • Insurance partners logo highlight to suggest wide range of coverage

Content Hierarchy

I observed in various tealeaf sessions, user was struggling to find relevent information. While buying a insurance user want to see value of insurance like what they are covered for, how much they are covered for or what is not included. I re-organize the content to have more logical sequence.

  • Clear and concise coverage details for user
  • Iconography to highlight coverage
  • Strategical placement of CTA for easy access and motivation

Section Highlights

While talking to sales agents we learned that users don't understand what kind of insurance and how much coverage they need for their business. This misunderstanding sometime cost them and they end up buying wrong cover or coverage amount. This information is available on page but lack visibility.

  • Addressing user confusion regarding cover amount
  • Strategical placement of CTA for easy access and motivation
  • The highlighted section is easy to spot and helps users scan pages without getting confused

Results & Impact

We monitored landingpage performance for next one month after the launch. The adaptive mobile experience led to measurable improvements in user satisfaction and business metrics. Key performance indicators showed substantial growth following the implementation.
