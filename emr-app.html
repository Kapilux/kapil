EMR App- Empowering Healthcare Professionals

App designed to streamline workflows and enhance communication for doctors within hospital

2 weeks
Lead UX Designer
Web | Tab

Overview

The Doctors EMR App is envisioned as a comprehensive digital tool designed to streamline workflows and enhance communication for doctors within hospital settings. The core idea revolves around enabling seamless digital interaction between doctors, patients, staff, and colleagues. The app aims to facilitate efficient tracking of patient health conditions and medication, providing doctors with up-to-date information to improve patient care. Its user-friendly interface prioritizes simplicity and quick access to crucial data.

My Role: I was part of the project as a Lead product designer and was responsible for UX/UI Design.

Research-Driven Insights

The project was started to solve real pain points doctors faced with outdated systems and scattered communication.

  • Digital Transformation in Healthcare: Healthcare’s going through a big digital shift, which brings a lot of opportunities—but also some real challenges. With so many players driving innovation, there’s a growing need for flexible, future-ready solutions that can keep up.
  • High Patient Volume and Doctor Overload: Doctors are under constant pressure with loads of patients and not enough time. Keeping track of everything physically and staying on top of every case can get really tough.
  • Need for Efficient Data Management: With patient info getting more complex, doctors need clean, easy-to-access digital records instead of dealing with messy, outdated paper systems.
  • Importance of Seamless Communication: Good communication between doctors, staff, and specialists is key to great patient care, but the current systems can be clunky and slow things down.

To gain comprehensive insights, a multi-faceted user research approach was employed:

User Research Methods

Medical Staff Interviews

We had in-depth, semi-structured chats with a mix of medical staff to understand their day-to-day work, the pain points they face with current systems (like paper records and EMRs), where was lack of communication, and what they’d want in an ideal solution.

Stakeholders Interview

We also spoke with key stakeholders get a broader perspective on app's approach. These conversations helped us understand things like business priorities, tech limitations, data security worries, and how the app could improve hospital efficiency and patient care.

Competitive Analysis

We reviewed some of the EMR systems and healthcare apps available, where they fall short, and how we could stand out. These insights helped guide our design decisions and made sure the Doctors EMR App brought something fresh and valuable to the table.

Major research insights and significant pain points within the current healthcare systems:

Pain Points

  • Difficulty in Quickly Assessing Patient Status: Doctors need a fast and intuitive way to understand the current condition of their patients.
  • Lack of Centralized and Quick Access to Patient Health Reports: Retrieving and reviewing patient history and vital information can be time-consuming.
  • Challenges in Locating Patients: Knowing the precise location (ward/bed number) of a patient is essential for timely intervention.
  • Limited Visibility and Connectivity with On-Site Colleagues: Doctors need to easily identify and connect with available colleagues or staff members near a patient, especially in urgent situations.
  • Inconvenience in Reviewing Diagnosis and Prescription Details: Accessing a patient's complete diagnostic history and medication list should be straightforward.
  • Inefficient Schedule Management: Doctors require a clear and readily available overview of their daily appointments and tasks.
  • Delayed Notifications in Emergency Situations: Timely alerts regarding critical patient events are vital for prompt response.
  • Difficulty in Accessing Medication Details: Comprehensive information about current and past medications is crucial for informed decision-making.
  • Lack of Streamlined Note-Taking and Information Sharing: Sharing patient-related notes and updates with other care team members needs improvement.
  • Ineffective Consultation and Communication Tracking: Consulting with colleagues and keeping track of those conversations can be disjointed.
  • Reliance on Paper-Based Records: Paper records are often inefficient, difficult to access, and prone to errors.

Ideation

Based on our research findings, we started on the ideation to create IA, wireframes and visual design. We iteratively refined these designs through multiple feedback sessions with stakeholders and users.

Journey Mapping

I created a journey map outlines the typical day of a staff and how they manage their daily tasks and patient care.

Approach

We started with a strong focus on addressing the pain points identified through user research, incorporating features such as:

Intelligent Dashboard with Prioritized Information
Dashboard with most critical information first, such as urgent alerts, upcoming critical appointments, and a summary of patients.
Unified Patient Profile with Timeline View
A centralized view of all patient information, including a chronological timeline of medical history, diagnoses, medications, and notes for easy comprehension.
Real-time Patient Location Tracking
Integration with hospital systems to provide accurate and up-to-date patient locations, potentially with visual mapping within the app.
Providing Relevant Information
Delivering the right information at the right time, tailored to the doctor's current task and the patient's specific situation.
Creating Insight in Patient Details and Schedule
Presenting complex data in a clear, visual, and easily digestible format to facilitate quick understanding and decision-making.
Accessible Anytime, Anyplace (Reliably and Securely)
Ensuring consistent and secure access to information across different devices and network conditions.
Transparent and Trackable
Maintaining a clear history of actions and communications for accountability and improved collaboration.
Keeping it Simple
Prioritizing ease of use and minimizing the learning curve to ensure rapid adoption and integration into daily workflows.

UI Design Phase

The wireframing and visual design processes were heavily influenced by user feedback, with iterative design and usability testing planned to ensure the app meets the needs and expectations of the target users. The focus is on creating an intuitive, efficient, and visually appealing interface that supports seamless workflows.

Patients List

Patients are the imperative to any doctor. This screen will provide insight of all available patients.

Quick view of Patient details

Patient quick view will allow the doctor to get a quick insight about the patient's condition without leaving the current screen.

  • Common information regarding the location of patient, ID, age, gander and blood group.

Patient Detail

If case more information is needed, doctor can visit detail page to get full information about patient. All information related to patient will be available in this section and easy to access. Tabs are introduced to navigate between different section.

  • Information about available doctors or medical staff nearby (if user is checking information while away from location)

Conclusion:

The Doctors EMR App is being developed with a deep understanding of the challenges and needs of healthcare professionals, gained through comprehensive user research. By directly addressing the identified pain points with a user-centric design approach, the app aims to be a valuable tool that enhances efficiency, improves communication, and ultimately contributes to better patient care within the demanding healthcare environment. The emphasis on seamless integration, robust security, and intuitive usability will be critical for successful adoption and long-term value.
